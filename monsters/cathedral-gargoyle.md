# Cathedral Gargoyle

*Large elemental, neutral evil*
![](/images/stat-block-header-bar.svg)

**Armor Class** 17 (natural armor)

**Hit Points** 180 ()

**Speed** 40 ft., climb 30 ft., fly 60 ft.

![](/images/stat-block-header-bar.svg)

**STR** 24 (+7) **DEX** 14 (+2) **CON** 22 (+6) **INT** 8 (-1) **WIS** 14 (+2) **CHA** 10 (+0)

![](/images/stat-block-header-bar.svg)

**Saving Throws** Str +12, Con +11, Wis +7

**Skills** Perception +7, Stealth +7

**Damage Resistances** bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing from nonmagical attacks that aren’t adamantine

**Damage Immunities** poison

**Damage weakness** lightning, cold

**Condition Immunities** exhaustion, petrified, poisoned

**Senses** darkvision 120 ft., tremorsense 60 ft., passive

**Perception** 17

**Languages** Terran, understands Common

**Challenge** 15 (13,000 XP)

**Proficiency Bonus** +5

![](/images/stat-block-header-bar.svg)

Legendary Resistance (3/Day). If the gargoyle fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead.
- Magic Resistance. The gargoyle has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.
- Magic Weapons. The gargoyle’s weapon attacks are magical.
- False Appearance. While the gargoyle remains motionless, it is indistinguishable from an inanimate statue.
- Stonebound Form. The gargoyle can move through difficult terrain made of stone or earth without expending extra movement. When it takes thunder damage, it has resistance to that instance of damage.
- Siege Monster. The gargoyle deals double damage to objects and structures.

![](/images/stat-block-header-bar.svg)

## Actions

- Multiattack. The gargoyle makes four attacks: one Bite, two Claws, and one Tail, or it makes three attacks and uses Wing Buffet if available.
- Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +12 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 20 (2d12 + 7) piercing damage.
- Claw. Melee Weapon Attack: +12 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 18 (2d10 + 7) slashing damage.
- Tail. Melee Weapon Attack: +12 to hit, reach 15 ft., one target. Hit: 18 (2d10 + 7) bludgeoning damage.
- Wing Buffet (Recharge 4–6). Each creature within 10 feet of the gargoyle must succeed on a DC 20 Dexterity saving throw or take 16 (2d8 + 7) bludgeoning damage, be pushed up to 10 feet, and knocked prone. On a success, a creature takes half damage and isn’t pushed or knocked prone. The gargoyle can then fly up to half its flying speed without provoking opportunity attacks.
- Shattering Roar (Recharge 5–6). The gargoyle unleashes a resonant thunderous roar in a 60-foot cone. Each creature in the area must make a DC 20 Constitution saving throw, taking 45 (10d8) thunder damage on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one. Creatures that fail the save are deafened until the end of their next turn. Objects and structures in the area take double damage.

## Bonus Actions

- Stone Glide. The gargoyle moves up to 20 feet, passing through up to 10 feet of stone or earthen material as though it were difficult terrain. It can’t end this movement inside a solid object.

## Reactions

- Hardened Hide. When the gargoyle is hit by an attack, it adds +3 to its AC against that attack, potentially causing it to miss. The gargoyle must be able to see the attacker to use this reaction.

## Legendary Actions

The gargoyle can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature’s turn. The gargoyle regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn.

- Detect. The gargoyle makes a Wisdom (Perception) check.
- Rend. The gargoyle makes one Claw attack.
- Hurl Rubble (Costs 2 Actions). Ranged Weapon Attack: +10 to hit, range 60 ft., one target. Hit: 18 (2d10 + 7) bludgeoning damage, and the target must succeed on a DC 20 Strength saving throw or be restrained by debris until the end of its next turn.
- Wing Surge (Costs 2 Actions). The gargoyle uses Wing Buffet if it’s available, even if it hasn’t recharged.

## Liar Actions

On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), while in or upon a stonework lair (cathedral, fortress, cave system), the gargoyle can use one of the following lair actions:

- Animate Masonry. Stonework within 30 feet becomes briefly animate. Each creature of the gargoyle’s choice in the area must succeed on a DC 20 Dexterity saving throw or take 10 (3d6) bludgeoning damage and be knocked prone.
- Echoing Roar. The lair amplifies sound. Until initiative count 20 of the next round, creatures other than the gargoyle have disadvantage on Perception checks relying on hearing, and the gargoyle’s thunder damage ignores resistance.
- Stone Seal. A door, window, or passage of stone within 60 feet seals shut. A creature adjacent to the feature can make a DC 20 Strength (Athletics) check as an action to break it open.
